1Ensure Safety and Call 911 Immediately

Emergency responders arriving at accident scene - always call 911 first

Your safety is the absolute priority. Even if the accident seems minor, always call 911. Here's why this step is non-negotiable:

  • Hidden injuries are common: Adrenaline can mask serious injuries like whiplash, concussions, or internal bleeding
  • Official police report: This creates an unbiased record of the accident that insurance companies and courts recognize
  • Traffic control: Police can manage traffic flow and prevent secondary accidents
  • Evidence preservation: Officers will document the scene before it's disturbed

üîç Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long do I have to file a car accident claim in California?

A: You have 2 years from the date of the accident to file a personal injury lawsuit. However, it's crucial to contact an attorney immediately to preserve evidence and protect your rights.

Q: What if I was partially at fault for the accident?

A: California follows "comparative negligence" laws. Even if you were partially at fault, you can still recover damages. For example, if you were 20% at fault and damages were $100,000, you could still recover $80,000.

Q: Should I accept the insurance company's first offer?

A: Never accept the first offer without consulting an attorney. Initial offers are typically 10-20% of the true case value. Insurance companies hope you'll settle quickly before understanding your rights.

Q: What if the other driver doesn't have insurance?

A: You may still have options through your own uninsured motorist coverage or by pursuing the other driver's personal assets. An experienced attorney can explore all available avenues for compensation.
